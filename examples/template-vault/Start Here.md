# Welcome

This vault is wired up for **Claude Notebook** — an AI study assistant that lives in the sidebar and works with the notes you're already writing. No setup needed. Here's how to use it.

## Open the panel

- Click the bot icon in the left ribbon, or press **Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+K**.
- The panel binds to whatever note you're currently viewing — open a lecture, and Claude already has it as context.

## The four modes

- **Chat** — ask questions, talk through a concept, get help thinking something through.
- **Edit** — ask Claude to revise or restructure the note you're viewing, directly.
- **Quiz** — turns the current note into a quick quiz to test yourself.
- **Ask vault** — searches across your *whole* vault, not just the open note. Use this when you can't remember which note something was in.

## The ✦ study presets

One click, no typing needed:

- **Practice Qs** — generate practice questions from the current note.
- **Flashcards** — turn key facts into spaced-repetition flashcards.
- **Explain simply** — plain-English rewrite of a dense concept.
- **Predict exam** — guess likely exam questions from the material.
- **Weak spots** — flag what you're shakiest on.
- **Mark my answer** — paste a draft answer and get it graded.
- **Synthesise across these** — combine multiple pinned notes into one view (see context tray below).
- **Find in my notes** — search your own notes for where a topic already appears.

## The context tray

Working across several lectures or subjects at once?

1. Click **+ Add note** to pin each relevant note into the tray.
2. Once you've pinned a few, run **Synthesise across these** to get a combined summary or comparison.

## Save your work

Anything useful Claude generates — practice questions, a summary, flashcards — click **Save as study note** and it's filed into `Study/`, cited back to the source note it came from.

## Drop files straight in

- Drag a **PDF or any file** anywhere into Obsidian and it's automatically converted, classified, and filed for you.
- **Paste an image** directly into a note and Claude transcribes it (handwritten notes, whiteboard photos, slides).

## Spaced review

Click **Show due reviews** any time to see the flashcards and study notes that are due today, pulled from everything you've saved.

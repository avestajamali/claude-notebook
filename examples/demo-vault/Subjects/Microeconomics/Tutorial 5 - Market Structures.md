## Why This Tutorial Matters

The classic exam task is to solve a monopolist's price and quantity, then compare it to the competitive outcome. Getting the marginal-revenue step right is where most marks are won or lost.

---

## Q1. Solve the Monopoly Outcome

A monopolist faces demand $P = 80 - 2Q$ with marginal cost $MC = 20$.

Revenue is $TR = (80 - 2Q)Q$, so $MR = 80 - 4Q$. Set $MR = MC$:

$$80 - 4Q = 20 \;\Rightarrow\; Q = 15, \quad P = 80 - 2(15) = \boxed{50}$$

The monopolist charges **\$50** and sells **15 units**. See [[Market Structures]] for the $MR = MC$ rule.

## Q2. Compare to Perfect Competition

Under perfect competition, price is driven to marginal cost: $P = MC = 20$. Set demand equal:

$$80 - 2Q = 20 \;\Rightarrow\; Q = 30$$

So competition gives \$20 and 30 units — half the price and double the quantity of monopoly.

> [!warning]
> Don't set $P = MC$ for a monopolist. A monopolist sets $MR = MC$; only a competitive firm sets $P = MC$. The size of the markup depends on [[Elasticity of Demand]].

## Key Takeaways
- [ ] Monopoly: $MR = MC$, giving $P > MC$.
- [ ] Competition: $P = MC$, lower price and higher quantity.
